Own VPN

When I was at my new school, I wanted a VPN because the school WiFi blocked many things. I made my first VPN with WireGuard, but since it uses the UDP protocol, which is blocked on our school network, I had to find another way. I installed PiVPN and configured OpenVPN to use TCP, which is not blocked. Now I have a VPN for my phone that bypasses the school's restrictions.

OpenVPN on Chromebook

Adding a VPN to a Chromebook sounds simple because you can download the OpenVPN app and import the configuration. However, I wanted the VPN on my school account, where I couldn’t install the app. I had to use the built-in VPN settings on the Chromebook. After importing the certificate and troubleshooting, I found a solution. For security reasons, I’ll update the details of this method on 01/02/2025.

Own NAS

A NAS (Network Attached Storage) is a versatile device for storing files, running virtual machines, or hosting Docker containers. I used TrueNAS Core to set up a NAS for backing up files from my PC and phone. Later, I switched to TrueNAS Scale for its better features, as it runs on Debian compared to Core's FreeBSD. I used an old laptop, and both versions are great for beginners due to their ease of use.

Own Dashboard

I created a personalized dashboard for monitoring and managing my projects. It integrates various tools and services I use, providing a centralized control panel. This project enhanced my understanding of front-end and back-end development.

Making a Reverse Proxy

A reverse proxy allows people to connect to your services without accessing your entire network. I used Cloudflare to set up my reverse proxy. It connects through a Raspberry Pi, ensuring that external users can access my apps securely without seeing all my devices.
